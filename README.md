## üìò Overview  
This project, **"Time Wasters on Social Media"**, explores how social media usage impacts **productivity, addiction levels, and behavioral patterns** among users.  
Using a **synthetic dataset** of 1,000 users, the analysis focuses on how demographics, income, device type, and content preferences influence total time spent and productivity loss.  

The project combines **data-driven insights** and **visual storytelling** to uncover how social media habits can shape modern digital behavior and overall well-being.

## üìà Key Analysis  

1. **Platform-Wise Usage Patterns**  
   - Facebook leads with ~26% of total usage.  
   - Instagram lowest at ~24%.  
   - YouTube and TikTok nearly equal (~25%).  
   - ‚áí *All platforms contribute equally to time-wasting.*

2. **Demographic Influences**  
   - Age and gender show minimal effect.  
   - **Lower income groups** show higher addiction and productivity loss.  
   - **Higher income groups** show better productivity and less addiction.  

3. **Device, OS & Connectivity Factors**  
   - Smartphones dominate usage (especially Android).  
   - Wi-Fi vs. Mobile Data has little effect ‚Äî smartphones simply allow constant access.  

4. **Content Engagement & Behavioral Insights**  
   - **Pranks & Vlogs** have the highest watch times (~16‚Äì17 mins).  
   - **Trends** have the shortest (~14 mins).  
   - Higher addiction correlates with lower productivity and more financial stress.  
   - Main motivators: **entertainment**, **habit**, and **procrastination**.

## üß† Conclusion  
Social media time-wasting is **driven by behavior and content**, not merely by technology or demographics.  
Addiction levels and productivity loss are strongest among users with **lower income and higher entertainment dependence**.  

The key to reducing time-wasting lies in **habit redesign**, **mindful consumption**, and **self-awareness** ‚Äî not just reducing screen time.
